# ICD-10-CM Codes for Neonatal Conditions

Diagnosis codes for common neonatal and perinatal conditions. Used in DG1-3 segments with system identifier `I10`.

> **Format in DG1-3**: `Code^Description^I10` — the `I10` system identifier tells the receiver this is ICD-10-CM.

---

## Respiratory

| ICD-10 Code | Description | Clinical Context |
|-------------|-------------|------------------|
| `P28.4` | Other apnea of newborn | Primary apnea of prematurity — the most common respiratory diagnosis in preterm infants |
| `P28.3` | Primary sleep apnea of newborn | Central apnea (distinguished from obstructive) |
| `P28.2` | Cyanotic attacks of newborn | Episodes of oxygen desaturation with visible cyanosis |
| `P28.5` | Respiratory failure of newborn | Broad category for respiratory insufficiency |
| `P28.89` | Other specified respiratory conditions of newborn | Catch-all for respiratory issues not otherwise classified |
| `P27.1` | Bronchopulmonary dysplasia originating in the perinatal period | BPD — chronic lung disease of prematurity, usually after prolonged ventilation |
| `P27.8` | Other chronic respiratory diseases originating in the perinatal period | |
| `P22.0` | Respiratory distress syndrome of newborn | RDS (hyaline membrane disease) — surfactant deficiency |
| `P22.1` | Transient tachypnea of newborn | TTN — usually resolves within 72 hours |
| `P25.1` | Pneumothorax originating in the perinatal period | Air leak syndrome |

**Coding note**: Prematurity and birth weight codes are often used as secondary diagnoses alongside the primary condition code. A baby with apnea of prematurity might carry both `P28.4` (primary) and `P07.33` (secondary).
